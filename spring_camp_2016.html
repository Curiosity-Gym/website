The spring camp schedule and description of sessions is below.

Digital StoryTelling

Creating stories is one of the most important activities of humans. It gives meaning to our other activities, and allows for sharing with all.
There are patterns in stories and almost all knowledge that we create, takes the form of stories. Some we believe in more than others.
During this workshop, we will engage in games, creative writing, critical thinking, conversations to uncover feelings and experiences of personal importance. Then using Scratch Software we will create digital stories that can be shared with the world.
Throughout the session we will discuss concepts from various storytelling traditions like arts, science, philosophy, technology and more.
Date : 29th Feb 2016

Make your own Smartphone Android App

Use MIT AppInventor to learn how to create functional apps that can use your own phones sensors.
Build your own global book search app or digital thermometer app.
Participants need to come with their Android Smartphones..
Date : 1st March 2016

Commanding Light

During this workshop we will be building a telescope and microscope to understand the nature of light, its behavior and how we can manipulate it to expand our sense of vision, so that we can see the farthest objects as well as the smallest things.
The kids get a Take away telescope at no additional cost.
Date : 2nd March 2016

Papercraft

The Art of Origami which originated in Japan comes from the word “Oru” meaning “folding” and “Kami” meaning “paper”.
Using a simple medium like paper, participants will learn the basics of Origami, how it has evolved and its progressive change as an method of expression.
Participants will also learn how to make complex geometric and 3D shapes like a “Hyperbolic Paraboloid” and a mobile object like dancing cubes made from strips of paper
Date : 3rd March 2016
Time : 9.30 a.m. to 12.30 p.m.

Make and Break

This session we will break/disassemble and understand the working of a gadget and then make/assemble a couple of gadgets.
Date : 3rd March 2016
Time : 1.30 p.m. to 4.30 p.m.

Build and Fly a pocket drone

This course is aimed at young flying enthusiasts who want to take their hobby and skill to the next level.
You will learn how a drone works, what are its parts, be able to construct the drone, and then successfully fly it. You will be provided with a kit to build, and guided on how to assemble the drone, which can be purchased for an additional cost.
Participants need to carry an Android phone with them, which will be used to install a certified application to control the drone.
Rs 5000 extra for a takeaway drone
Date : 4th March 2016
Participants should bring their own android phone.
